Workshop Organizers

Our organizing committee bringing together experts across geometry and foundation models

Chunchen Wang

Ph.D. Student, MBZUAI

Chunchen Wang is a Ph.D. student at MBZUAI. His research focuses on graph data mining and machine learning algorithms. As an active young researcher, he explores applications of graph neural networks in complex systems and maintains close academic collaborations with top international laboratories, regularly publishing at premier conferences in the field.

Karish Grover

Ph.D. Student, Carnegie Mellon University

Karish Grover is a Ph.D. student at Carnegie Mellon University. His research focuses on the co-design of machine learning systems and graph neural networks, with special attention to efficient learning algorithms for ultra-large-scale graph data. He works on solving computational and storage bottlenecks for billion-node graph data, with his research on scalable graph learning receiving significant attention at top conferences like KDD and NeurIPS.

Pietro Liò

Professor, University of Cambridge

Pietro Liò is a Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Technology at the University of Cambridge and a renowned expert in artificial intelligence and bioinformatics. His research primarily focuses on graph neural networks (GNNs), multimodal learning, and their applications in computational biology. As a proactive advocate for AI for Science, he is dedicated to developing advanced AI models to tackle complex problems in genomics and personalized medicine.

Philip S. Yu

Distinguished Professor, University of Illinois Chicago

Philip S. Yu is a Distinguished Professor and Wexler Chair Professor at the University of Illinois Chicago, and a Fellow of both ACM and IEEE. He has made pioneering contributions to graph mining, social network analysis, and deep learning. With exceptionally high academic impact, he has received numerous lifetime achievement awards including the SIGKDD Innovation Award and is ranked among the world's most highly cited computer scientists.

Marinka Zitnik

Associate Professor, Harvard University

Marinka Zitnik is an Associate Professor at Harvard Medical School, focusing on establishing AI foundations that can autonomously acquire scientific understanding to enhance drug development and personalized medicine. She is the co-founder of Therapeutics Data Commons, dedicated to promoting international collaboration in AI4Science. Her research focuses on creating AI models that can tackle complex biomedical problems with real-world impact.

Li Sun

Associate Professor, Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications

Li Sun is an Associate Professor at Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications. His main research directions include graph mining, Riemannian machine learning, and continual learning. He is committed to utilizing non-Euclidean geometry theory to solve the over-smoothing problem of graph neural networks, has won the CIKM 2022 Best Paper Award, and has conducted visiting research at the University of Illinois. His work bridges theoretical advances with practical applications in scientific domains.

Zhongtian Sun

Lecturer, University of Kent

Zhongtian Sun is a Lecturer at the University of Kent, having previously held research positions at both the University of Cambridge and the University of Oxford. His research focuses on explainable AI and graph representation learning. As a Mila AI Policy Scholar, he is also dedicated to financial technology applications and AI security policy research, working to ensure AI systems are transparent, fair, and beneficial to society.
