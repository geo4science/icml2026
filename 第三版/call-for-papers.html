Call for Papers

Submit your research on geometric foundation models

Workshop Overview

The Workshop on Geometric Foundation Models (GeoFM) aims to bring together researchers from geometric deep learning, foundation models, and scientific computing to address fundamental challenges at their intersection. While foundation models have revolutionized many domains, their application to scientific data with inherent geometric structure remains challenging. This workshop focuses on how to effectively incorporate geometric priors—such as symmetries, invariances, and physical constraints—into foundation models for scientific applications.

Topics of Interest

We welcome submissions on a broad range of topics related to geometric foundation models

Equivariant & Invariant Architectures

  • SE(3)-equivariant foundation models
  • Manifold-valued representation learning
  • Group-theoretic approaches to geometric deep learning
  • Multi-scale geometric representations
  • Conformal and projective geometric models

Scientific Applications

  • Geometric foundation models for molecular design
  • Materials discovery with symmetry-aware models
  • Climate and earth science modeling
  • Computational biology and protein engineering
  • Neuroscience and brain connectivity analysis

Foundation Model Techniques

  • Pretraining strategies for geometric data
  • Transfer learning across geometric domains
  • Parameter-efficient fine-tuning of geometric models
  • Sparse and efficient geometric attention
  • Self-supervised learning with geometric constraints

Graph & Mesh Foundation Models

  • Large-scale graph foundation models
  • Mesh transformers and geometric neural networks
  • Heterogeneous geometric data fusion
  • Dynamic and temporal geometric modeling
  • Scalable inference for geometric foundation models

Evaluation & Benchmarking

  • Benchmarks for geometric reasoning
  • Evaluating physical plausibility in generative models
  • Metrics for geometric understanding in foundation models
  • Dataset curation for scientific geometry
  • Fair comparison of geometric learning approaches

Theory & Principles

  • Theoretical limits of geometry-agnostic foundation models
  • Sample complexity with and without geometric priors
  • Information-theoretic perspectives on geometric learning
  • Connections between physics-informed ML and geometric DL
  • Geometric perspectives on model generalization

Submission Guidelines

  • Paper Format: Maximum 8 pages excluding references and appendices, using ICML 2025 template
  • Review Process: Double-blind review by at least 3 expert reviewers from our program committee
  • Non-Archival: Accepted papers will be posted on the workshop website but will not appear in the official ICML proceedings, allowing resubmission to archival venues
  • Open Source: We encourage authors to release code and data alongside their submissions
  • Ethical Considerations: All submissions must include a discussion of potential societal impact and limitations
