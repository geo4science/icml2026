Geo4S: Geometric Foundation Models for Science Discovery Workshop

Unifying Geometric Deep Learning and Foundation Models for Scientific Discovery

Overview

Exploring the intersection of geometric deep learning and foundation models for scientific applications

The motivation for this workshop builds on the rapid progress of foundation models and their growing impact on AI4Science. Geometric structures, such as graphs in biochemical molecules, helical arrangements in proteins and DNA, and crystalline lattices, are central to many high-impact applications in the life and material sciences. While recent advances, including AlphaFold3 and early adaptations of large language models to scientific data, demonstrate the promise of this direction, foundation models for geometric structures remain at an early stage. In particular, core geometric principles, most notably symmetry-aware modeling through equivariant and conformal representations, are not yet systematically leveraged, leaving fundamental questions around generality, data efficiency, interpretability, and trustworthiness unresolved. We are committed to Delving into the Microworld through Riemannian Geometry
